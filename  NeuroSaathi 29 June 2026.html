My son is 3 and still not talking. Should I be worried?
Thank you for reaching out. Speech delay in a 3-year-old can have several causes. When did he say his first words — and has he lost any words he previously had?
He said mama and papa at 12 months but stopped around 18 months.
This pattern — losing words after 18 months — is an important sign. I strongly recommend a formal developmental assessment and speech therapy evaluation without delay. Early intervention makes a significant difference.

📔 Food Diary

🥛 Dairy-Free Options

  • Ragi (calcium-rich)
  • Sesame seeds (til)
  • Calcium-fortified rice milk
  • Green leafy vegetables
  • Tofu & soy products
  • Almonds & chia seeds

💪 Protein (Vegetarian)

  • All dals (moong, masoor, toor)
  • Paneer (if tolerated)
  • Rajma & chana
  • Soya chunks & tofu
  • Quinoa & nuts
  • Peanut butter

🧠 Brain Foods

  • Walnuts & flaxseeds (Omega-3)
  • Blueberries (antioxidants)
  • Turmeric (anti-inflammatory)
  • Leafy greens (folate)
  • Pumpkin seeds (zinc)
  • Eggs (choline)

⚠️ Foods to Limit

  • Artificial food colors (tartrazine)
  • Preservatives (BHA, BHT)
  • High-sugar drinks & candy
  • Maida-heavy foods
  • Packaged chips & snacks
  • MSG in processed food

🍱 Easy Indian Meals

  • Khichdi (nutritious & calming)
  • Idli/Dosa (gluten-free)
  • Dal + rice (complete protein)
  • Poha (iron-rich)
  • Ragi porridge (calcium)
  • Sabudana khichdi (light)

💊 Key Supplements

  • Vitamin D3 (very common deficiency)
  • Vitamin B12 (esp. vegetarians)
  • Omega-3 EPA/DHA
  • Zinc (behavior & immunity)
  • Magnesium (sleep & calm)
  • Probiotics (gut-brain axis)
⚠️ Disclaimer: Dietary suggestions are general in nature based on publicly available research. Consult a registered dietitian or doctor before significant dietary changes, especially elimination diets or supplements.

🆘 Meltdown Emergency Guide

1

Stay calm. Your calm is contagious. Take a breath first.

2

Remove the trigger if possible. Move to a quieter space.

3

Reduce demands. Don't reason or teach during a meltdown.

4

Give space. Use minimal, soft words only.

5

Stay nearby for safety. Wait for the storm to pass.

6

Offer comfort after — hug, water, their comfort object.

🚨 If child is in immediate danger — call 112
