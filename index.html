My son is 3 and still not talking. Should I be worried?
Thank you for reaching out. Speech delay in a 3-year-old can have several causes. When did he say his first words — and has he lost any words he previously had?
He said mama and papa at 12 months but stopped around 18 months.
This pattern — losing words after 18 months — is an important sign. I strongly recommend a formal developmental assessment and speech therapy evaluation without delay. Early intervention makes a significant difference.

📔 Food Diary

🥛 Dairy-Free Options

  • Ragi (calcium-rich)
  • Sesame seeds (til)
  • Calcium-fortified rice milk
  • Green leafy vegetables
  • Tofu & soy products
  • Almonds & chia seeds

💪 Protein Sources

  • All dals (moong, masoor, toor)
  • Paneer (if tolerated)
  • Rajma & chana
  • Soya chunks & tofu
  • Quinoa & nuts
  • Peanut butter

🧠 Brain Foods

  • Walnuts & flaxseeds (Omega-3)
  • Blueberries (antioxidants)
  • Turmeric (anti-inflammatory)
  • Leafy greens (folate)
  • Pumpkin seeds (zinc)
  • Amla (Vitamin C)

🍱 Easy Veg Meals

  • Khichdi (nutritious & calming)
  • Idli/Dosa (gluten-free option)
  • Dal + rice (complete protein)
  • Poha (iron-rich)
  • Ragi porridge (calcium)
  • Sabudana khichdi (light)

⚠️ Foods to Limit

  • Artificial food colors (tartrazine)
  • Preservatives (BHA, BHT)
  • High-sugar drinks & candy
  • Maida-heavy foods
  • Packaged chips & snacks
  • MSG in processed food

💊 Key Supplements

  • Vitamin D3 (very common deficiency)
  • Vitamin B12 (essential for veg)
  • Omega-3 from flaxseed/algae
  • Zinc (behavior & immunity)
  • Magnesium (sleep & calm)
  • Probiotics (gut-brain axis)

🥚 Egg Benefits

  • Eggs — excellent choline source (brain health)
  • High quality complete protein
  • Vitamin D (naturally present)
  • B12 (essential for brain)
  • Selenium & iodine
  • Easy to prepare for children

💪 Protein Sources

  • Eggs (boiled, scrambled, omelette)
  • All dals and legumes
  • Paneer & cottage cheese
  • Greek yogurt (if dairy ok)
  • Soya chunks & tofu
  • Rajma & chickpeas

🧠 Brain Foods

  • Eggs (choline for brain development)
  • Walnuts & flaxseeds (Omega-3)
  • Blueberries (antioxidants)
  • Turmeric in cooking
  • Leafy greens (folate)
  • Pumpkin seeds (zinc)

🍱 Easy Egg Meals

  • Egg bhurji with roti
  • Boiled eggs with dal rice
  • Egg fried rice (less oil)
  • Veggie omelette
  • Egg curry (mild for children)
  • Egg paratha (protein rich)

⚠️ Foods to Limit

  • Fried eggs (excess oil)
  • Processed egg products
  • Artificial food colors
  • High-sugar drinks & candy
  • Packaged chips & snacks
  • MSG in processed food

💊 Key Supplements

  • Vitamin D3 (eggs help but not enough)
  • Omega-3 EPA/DHA (from fish oil or algae)
  • Zinc (behavior & immunity)
  • Magnesium (sleep & calm)
  • Probiotics (gut-brain axis)
  • Iron if needed (check blood test)

🐟 Best Proteins

  • Fish (salmon, rohu, pomfret) — Omega-3
  • Chicken (lean, boiled/grilled)
  • Eggs — daily if possible
  • Turkey (tryptophan for mood)
  • Avoid processed meats
  • Choose fresh over packaged

🧠 Omega-3 Rich Foods

  • Fatty fish — salmon, sardines, rohu
  • Fish 2-3 times per week ideal
  • Walnuts & flaxseeds (plant omega-3)
  • Fish oil supplements if needed
  • Omega-3 linked to better focus
  • Anti-inflammatory benefits

🧠 Brain Foods

  • Fish (DHA for brain development)
  • Eggs (choline)
  • Chicken liver (B12, iron, folate)
  • Turmeric in cooking
  • Leafy greens
  • Blueberries & walnuts

🍱 Easy Meals for Kids

  • Mild fish curry with rice
  • Chicken khichdi (easy to eat)
  • Egg bhurji with roti
  • Grilled chicken with veggies
  • Fish with dal rice
  • Chicken soup (calming)

⚠️ Foods to Avoid

  • Processed meats (salami, sausages)
  • Fried chicken & fish
  • Fast food burgers
  • Artificial colors & preservatives
  • High mercury fish (shark, swordfish)
  • Excess red meat

💊 Key Supplements

  • Vitamin D3 (still needed)
  • Omega-3 supplement if not eating fish
  • Probiotics (gut-brain axis)
  • Magnesium (sleep & calm)
  • Zinc (behavior & immunity)
  • B12 from animal foods ✅
Disclaimer: Dietary suggestions are general in nature based on publicly available research. Always consult a registered dietitian or pediatrician before significant dietary changes, especially elimination diets or supplements for your child.

🆘 Meltdown Emergency Guide

1

Stay calm. Your calm is contagious. Take a breath first.

2

Remove the trigger if possible. Move to a quieter space.

3

Reduce demands. Don't reason or teach during a meltdown.

4

Give space. Use minimal, soft words only.

5

Stay nearby for safety. Wait for the storm to pass.

6

Offer comfort after — hug, water, their comfort object.

🚨 If child is in immediate danger — call 112
